Overview of the Netherlands


The Netherlands is known for its picturesque landscapes, vibrant cities, and rich history. From the iconic windmills and tulip fields to the charming canals of Amsterdam, there is so much to explore!

The Netherlands, also known as Holland, is a country in Northwestern Europe, with overseas territories in the Caribbean. It is the largest of the four constituent countries of the Kingdom of the Netherlands and consists of twelve provinces. The official language is Dutch, with West Frisian as a secondary language in the province of Friesland. The Netherlands is known for its low elevation and flat topography, with 26% below sea level. The country has a rich history, including the Dutch Golden Age, which saw the establishment of colonies and trading posts worldwide. With a population of over 18 million, the Netherlands is the world's second-largest exporter of food and agricultural products. The country has a parliamentary constitutional monarchy with a unitary structure since 1848 and has a long record of social tolerance, legalizing prostitution and euthanasia. The Netherlands is a founding member of various organizations, including the European Union, Eurozone, G10, NATO, OECD, and WTO. The Hague holds the seat of the States General, Cabinet, and Supreme Court.
